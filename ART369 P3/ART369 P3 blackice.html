Black Ice
Directed by Maarten van Rouveroy

Netherlands, 53 minutes

When the Greenpeace ship Arctic Sunrise set sail to protest the first ever oil drilling in the Arctic Ocean, none of the people on board could have known what was coming. Seized at gunpoint by Russian special forces, the ‘Arctic 30’ were thrust into headlines all over the world, facing up to 15 years in prison and finding themselves at the centre of a bitter international dispute. Their imprisonment, which saw worldwide media cast the Arctic 30 in the same mould as political prisoners like Pussy Riot and Mikhail Khodorkovsky, lasted months. However, their resolve to try and stop oil drilling in the Arctic was never broken.

Polar Bear Man
Directed by Wesley Enzinna

USA, 34 minutes

In July, 2013, Matt Dyer went on a Sierra-Club sponsored expedition to Canada’s Torngat Mountains where he was savagely mauled by a polar bear. Polar Bear Man explores the reasons behind Dyer’s attack, his unlikely survival, and his return to the Torngats one year later. Is climate change pushing starving polar bears to attack humans?
